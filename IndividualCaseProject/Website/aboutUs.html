About Me: The Current only Dev member

I am an aspiring .NET developer, and in my free time I work on a gaming project. I am learning 3D modeling with Blender and utilizing it for animation as well. I use Clip Studio Paint to get my ideas on paper, then take a crack at creating them. When trying to be an indie developer for games you basically have two engines to choose from. Unreal Engine, or Unity. I found Unreal Engine to be massive and difficult to find a starting point. Unity is also huge, however C# is more forgiving for a new programmer and with my work place also utilizing C# and .NET practices I decided that I would switch and start the project that way.

About the Project: Jaiger

The game I am going for will be a platformer with Weapon and skill mechanics. The game will have a level system based on the amount of times you use a weapon. It will also have skills that you gain after using other skills so many times. Your skills will level differently than your weapon and you will have a choice on where to spend your points. Although skill grinding will also affect your skills and the proficiency you have will be heavily dependent on usage. That being said, I want this to be more of a casual game than like Dark Souls or any game that makes your Grind. I just want you to notice a difference in the animations and attack power as you progress through the game. I mean, who likes a hero that never gets any better? Kinda boring right?
